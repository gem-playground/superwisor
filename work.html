  • Part 1 - Superwisor Rises


    Premise

    The first game prepares the supervisor for handling the first meeting with their DBA or PhD student. In this initial meeting, the relationship between the supervisor and his supervisee is established. Personalities, expectations and the research question are the core subjects of this game.


    Video Excerpt



    Dimensions

    1. Research Question - Demonstrate interest in research question and depth of knowledge in the research topic (Knowledge)
    2. State of the Art - Encourage exploring the state-of-the-art and provide leads for doing so (Knowledge)
    3. Expressing Interest - Illustrate enthusiasm about working with the student and express interest in their person beyond your working relationship (Behavior)
    4. Openness - Be open to new ideas and approaches from the student (Behavior)
    5. Expectations - Manage student expectations and clearly outline the milestones and deliverables of the research (Skill)
    6. Meetings - Arrange regular meetings and allow for flexible interaction (Skill)


  • Part 2 - The Return of the Research Proposal


    Premise

    The second game prepares the supervisor for helping the PhD or DBA students in traversing their first main deliverable: the extended research proposal. This meeting is the first place in which the supevisor must give the student critical and significant written feedback. Research methodology, working styles and feedback are all discussed in this game.


    Video Excerpt



    Dimensions

    1. Drive - Identify the studentâ€™s drive and motivation behind approaching the research in a specific manner (Knowledge)
    2. Methodology - Provide guidance on methodology based on knowledge of the field and research question (Knowledge)
    3. Influence - Influence the direction of the research while maintaining student autonomy (Behavior)
    4. Reflection - Encourage the student to reflect on decisions that he has made and reevaluate biases (Behavior)
    5. Feedback - Provide useful and meaningful written feedback that is actionable (Skill)
    6. Identify Research Community - Connect the student to relevant stakeholders in the research community that can provide guidance, resources, or relevant knowledge to his approach (Skill)
